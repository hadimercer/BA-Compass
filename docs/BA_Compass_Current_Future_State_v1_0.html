1. Purpose

This document presents a structured gap analysis comparing how a junior-to-mid BA practitioner navigates a new engagement today (current state) versus how they will navigate it with BA Compass (future state). It establishes the specific gaps that BA Compass closes, providing the evidentiary basis for the product's problem statement and validating that the solution is grounded in a real, observable need.

This analysis serves two purposes within the portfolio: it demonstrates rigorous problem definition (a core BA competency), and it makes the case to hiring managers and portfolio reviewers that BA Compass is a response to a genuine gap — not a technology demonstration in search of a problem.

Scope of analysis: The current state describes a junior BA (0–3 years experience) working on a new engagement without a dedicated senior mentor, using only standard office tools and their own training. The future state describes the same practitioner using BA Compass V1 as their primary engagement workspace.

2. Current State — How a Junior BA Navigates a New Engagement Today

When a junior BA is assigned a new engagement today, they face a sequence of high-stakes decisions with limited structured support. Each decision point is an opportunity for wasted effort, inconsistent outputs, or missed deliverables.

2.1 The Typical Journey — Current State

⚠️

Current State — Pain Points by Stage

Engagement Start

✕Assigned an engagement with a brief that may be ambiguous, incomplete, or framed as a solution rather than a problem
✕No structured intake process — relies on ad-hoc stakeholder conversations and intuition to define scope
✕Engagement type not formally classified — may proceed as if all engagements require the same deliverables
✕No mechanism to capture the 10 critical intake dimensions systematically

Planning

✕Roadmap built from scratch each time — usually a Word doc or Excel spreadsheet, often copied from a previous engagement
✕BABOK knowledge areas may not be systematically considered — common to under-plan elicitation or skip solution evaluation
✕No distinction between engagement types — a process improvement engagement and a requirements definition engagement get the same generic plan
✕Effort estimation unreliable because scope is not tied to a module inventory

Execution

✕Blank-page anxiety on each deliverable — no guided prompts, no structure, no examples
✕Output quality varies by deliverable — some thorough, some thin, depending on familiarity
✕Heavy reliance on templates downloaded from the internet — often not aligned to the specific engagement type or BABOK
✕Context not carried forward between deliverables — same background repeated, or context lost
✕No AI assistance — time spent on formatting and structure rather than analysis

Quality & Delivery

✕Gaps only discovered at stakeholder review — creates rework cycles and erodes credibility
✕No traceability matrix — requirements cannot be traced to deliverables without manual effort
✕Deliverable package assembled manually — inconsistent formatting, naming, and structure across documents
✕No objective signal of completeness — "done" is a feeling, not a verified state
✅

Future State — With BA Compass

Engagement Start

✓AI-led conversational intake systematically extracts all 10 dimensions across 3 tiers — no critical context missed
✓Engagement classified into one of 8 types × 3 scale tiers — with AI reasoning visible and user override available
✓Classification is persistent and drives every subsequent decision — roadmap, module selection, AI prompts
✓All intake dimensions stored and referenced throughout the engagement — context never lost

Planning

✓BABOK-aligned roadmap generated instantly from one of 24 pre-built templates matched to engagement type and scale
✓All 52 modules considered — none missed by accident. Each tagged by BABOK knowledge area
✓Roadmap is a living object — status tracked, modules navigable in any order, dependencies surfaced
✓Scope is tied to a defined module inventory — basis for realistic effort conversation with stakeholders

Execution

✓Co-pilot guided prompts eliminate blank-page anxiety — methodology embedded in the questions asked
✓AI drafts artifact content; user reviews and owns — consistent quality floor across all deliverables
✓Project context (dimensions, prior artifacts) automatically loaded into every co-pilot session
✓Iterative refinement loop within each module — user can push back, ask for alternatives, refine content
✓Time shifted from formatting to analysis — AI handles structure, BA applies judgement

Quality & Delivery

✓Gap analysis runs before delivery — AI reviews all artifacts against engagement requirements and flags weaknesses
✓Traceability matrix generated automatically — requirements traceable to deliverables at click of a button

✓Completeness is verified, not felt — gap analysis provides an objective signal before the package leaves the BA's hands

3. Gap Analysis — Capability by Capability

The table below lists each identified capability gap, the current state behaviour, the future state behaviour enabled by BA Compass, and the specific product feature that closes the gap.

Gap Area Current State Future State BA Compass Feature
G-01
Engagement Classification		 No formal classification. Practitioner proceeds with a generic approach regardless of engagement type. Process improvement treated the same as system requirements. Every project is classified into one of 8 engagement types at 3 scale tiers. Classification is AI-reasoned, explained, and user-overridable. Intake & Classification Engine (FR-08 to FR-13)
G-02
Intake Dimension Capture		 Intake is ad hoc — driven by whatever questions the BA thinks to ask. Critical dimensions (regulatory constraints, political sensitivity, data availability) routinely missed. 10 intake dimensions across 3 tiers captured conversationally. AI surfaces questions the BA may not have thought to ask. All dimensions persisted and referenced throughout the engagement. Conversational Intake (FR-08, FR-09, FR-10)
G-03
Roadmap Generation		 Roadmap built manually from scratch or copied from a previous engagement. BABOK coverage is inconsistent — common to miss elicitation planning or solution evaluation. Instant BABOK-aligned roadmap from 24 pre-built templates matched to engagement type and scale. All 52 modules considered. Nothing missed by accident. Roadmap Template Library + Instantiation (FR-14 to FR-16)
G-04
Module Navigation		 No structured module concept. Deliverables appear ad hoc on a task list. Dependencies between deliverables are invisible — a BA may produce a solution design before completing requirements. 52 modules navigable in any order. Dependency warnings surface when a module is opened before its predecessors are complete. Status tracked per module. Roadmap Navigator (FR-17, FR-18)
G-05
Artifact Creation Guidance		 Blank page for every deliverable. BA either writes from memory, uses a generic internet template, or copies from a prior engagement. Quality is highly variable. Co-pilot guided prompts for every module. AI asks the right questions in the right sequence, drawing on project context. Draft artifact produced as a starting point — BA edits, refines, owns. Co-Pilot Module Experience (FR-19 to FR-22)
G-06
Context Continuity		 Each deliverable created in isolation. BA must manually re-establish context — re-reading the brief, prior deliverables, and notes before starting each new document. Context drift common. All project dimensions and prior artifact content loaded automatically into every co-pilot session. AI has full engagement context. No context drift. Context Injection in Co-Pilot (FR-21)
G-07
Pre-Delivery Gap Check		 Gaps discovered at stakeholder review — after effort has been invested and credibility is at stake. No systematic self-review mechanism available to the BA before submission. AI-driven gap analysis reviews all artifacts against engagement requirements before delivery. Specific weaknesses flagged with explanations. BA addresses or explicitly acknowledges each flag. Gap Analysis Module (FR-25, FR-26)
G-08
Requirements Traceability		 Traceability matrix either not produced or produced manually at the end of the engagement — a time-consuming exercise that is frequently skipped under time pressure. Traceability matrix generated automatically from the project's artifact record. Requirements are traceable to deliverables without manual effort. Traceability Matrix Generation (FR-27)
G-09
Deliverable Package Quality		 Final package assembled manually. Documents may use different templates, naming conventions, and formatting. Package looks like it was produced by several different people. Export package compiled from all saved artifacts with consistent formatting and naming. Single download, professional presentation. Completeness Signal		 "Done" is a subjective feeling. BA submits when they run out of time or patience — no objective completeness check available. Gap analysis provides an objective completeness signal. BA knows what has been flagged and what has been addressed before the package leaves their hands. 4. Gap Severity Assessment

Each gap is rated by severity — the degree to which the absence of the capability impairs the quality of BA work and the probability that a junior practitioner will encounter the problem on a real engagement.

G-01 — Engagement Classification Critical
Foundation error — wrong classification cascades into wrong roadmap, wrong deliverables, and wrong scope expectations. The most consequential gap in the set.
G-02 — Intake Dimension Capture Critical
Missed dimensions (regulatory constraints, political sensitivity, integration complexity) create scope surprises late in the engagement — expensive to recover from.
G-05 — Artifact Creation Guidance Critical
The most time-consuming and anxiety-producing gap in daily practice. Every deliverable starts from zero. Disproportionate time spent on structure rather than analysis.
G-07 — Pre-Delivery Gap Check High
Gaps found at review stage damage credibility and create rework. A pre-delivery check that a junior BA can run independently is high-value and currently unavailable.
G-03 — Roadmap Generation High
Generic roadmaps miss engagement-specific requirements. A process improvement engagement without an as-is analysis module, or a regulatory engagement without a compliance review module, will fail scope.
G-06 — Context Continuity High
Context drift between deliverables is a common source of inconsistency. Stakeholders notice when the BA appears to re-explain background that was established weeks earlier.
G-10 — Completeness Signal High
Subjective "doneness" is a significant confidence risk for junior practitioners. An objective signal — even a simple one — meaningfully reduces anxiety and improves submission quality.
G-04 — Module Navigation Medium
Dependency violations are common but recoverable. The risk is wasted effort, not engagement failure. High value for complex engagements; lower value for small-scale work.
G-08 — Requirements Traceability Medium
Critical for formal or regulated engagements; less critical for advisory or strategy work. Automating it removes a significant time burden when it is required.
G-09 — Deliverable Package Quality Medium
Inconsistent formatting creates a negative impression but rarely causes engagement failure. High value for external-facing or formal deliverable contexts.

5. What BA Compass Closes vs. What It Does Not

This section provides an honest boundary on the product's scope. Not every gap in BA practice is in scope for V1. Clear scope boundaries are a sign of rigorous problem definition — and they protect against scope creep during the build phase.

Capability Area V1 Status Notes
Engagement classification Fully Closed — V1 8 engagement types × 3 scale tiers; AI-reasoned; user override
Intake dimension capture Fully Closed — V1 All 10 dimensions; 3-tier capture model; persisted throughout engagement
BABOK-aligned roadmap generation Fully Closed — V1 24 pre-built templates; 52 modules; instant generation on classification
Artifact creation guidance (co-pilot) Fully Closed — V1 Guided Q&A per module; AI drafts; iterative refinement; context injection
Pre-delivery gap analysis Fully Closed — V1 AI reviews all artifacts against engagement requirements; flags weaknesses
Requirements traceability Fully Closed — V1 Auto-generated traceability matrix from artifact record
Deliverable package export Fully Closed — V1 Consistent formatting; single/full package export; download link
Stakeholder collaboration Out of Scope — V1 V1 is solo-user only. Multi-user collaboration is a post-V1 feature.
Live stakeholder elicitation support Out of Scope — V1 BA Compass supports artifact production, not live workshop facilitation or meeting capture.
Integration with external tools (JIRA, Confluence) Out of Scope — V1 Export is file-based in V1. Integration with external project tools is post-V1.
Custom module creation / editing Out of Scope — V1 Module library is static in V1. UI for adding or editing modules is post-V1.
Estimation and scheduling Out of Scope — V1 BA Compass scopes work into modules but does not produce effort estimates or project schedules.

6. Summary — The Case for BA Compass

The analysis above documents ten distinct capability gaps in how a junior BA practitioner approaches a new engagement today. Seven of those gaps are rated High or Critical severity — meaning they routinely impair output quality or are encountered on virtually every engagement.

BA Compass closes all seven high/critical gaps within V1 scope. It does so not by adding a layer of AI on top of existing tools, but by restructuring the engagement workflow around a methodology engine that makes the right BA practice the path of least resistance. The AI is the accelerant; the BABOK methodology is the architecture.

The three gaps rated Medium severity (module navigation, traceability, package quality) are also addressed in V1 — they represent incremental quality improvements rather than fundamental capability unlocks, but they round out the product into a complete engagement workspace rather than a point solution.

The out-of-scope items (collaboration, live elicitation support, external integrations, custom modules) are genuine limitations, not oversights. They represent a deliberate V1 boundary that keeps the product buildable, testable, and demonstrable within a portfolio timeline — while leaving a clear and credible roadmap for future development.
